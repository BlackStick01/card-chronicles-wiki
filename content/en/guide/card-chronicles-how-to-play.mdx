## The Basics of Card Chronicles

Before you dive into the depths of this game, it's important to understand the basics. Card Chronicles is a game for 3-6 players, with a playtime of approximately 30 minutes. The game is designed for players aged 10 and up, making it an excellent choice for family game nights or casual gaming sessions.

### How to Win

The objective of Card Chronicles is to collect 3 Fame points. These points are earned by fulfilling the victory conditions of the History cards, which are revealed at the beginning of each round. The game is a mix of strategy, chance, and a bit of luck, as you'll need to manage your hand, your Allies, and the ever-changing game board.

## Setting Up the Game

To start a game of Card Chronicles, you'll need to set up the deck and distribute the cards. Here's a step-by-step guide:

1. Shuffle the History cards into a deck and turn over the top card. This card will serve as the theme for the round.
2. Each player receives a number of cards from the main deck based on the number of players. For example, in a 3-player game, each player receives 7 cards, while in a 6-player game, each player receives 5 cards.
3. The player who has the card matching the theme card revealed in step 1 reveals it and becomes the start player.

## Game Play

Each round of Card Chronicles is played over various tricks, known as 'meetings'. The lead player plays a card from their hand, and players take turns following suit or playing a Wildcard. The player who wins the trick takes all the cards and adds them to their 'Allies'.

### Key Gameplay Mechanics

- **Lead Card**: The lead player chooses any card from their hand to start the trick.
- **Suit Following**: Players must follow the suit of the lead card or play a Wildcard.
- **Special Abilities**: Cards played face up activate their special abilities, which can change the outcome of the trick.
- **Allies**: The cards won in a trick are added to the player's 'Allies' and can have various effects.

## Round End and Scoring

At the end of each round, players with cards left in hand add them to their Allies, and any 'End of Round' effects on Allies are activated. The player with the most 'Evil' Allies is excluded from scoring in the round. Players who fulfill the victory conditions of the History card gain 1 Fame point. If a player has all 4 Evil cards in their Allies, they gain 2 Fame points, and all other scoring is ignored.

## Game End

The game ends when a player reaches 3 Fame points. If multiple players reach 3 Fame on the same turn, the game continues until the tie is broken.
